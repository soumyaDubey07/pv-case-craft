Tips to keep in mind

  • Start with patient & indication - age, sex, drug, dose, route, start date.
  • Keep clear chronology - drug start -> event -> action -> outcome.
  • Be factual - describe what happened and when; avoid opinions.
  • Add key labs - include value, unit, and date.
  • State actions - stopped, reduced, or continued, with date.
  • Mention dechallenge/rechallenge - improved, unchanged, or not reported.
  • Include history & concomitants - note possible confounders.
  • End with reporter info - type, seriousness, and expectedness.
  • Write neutrally - third person, short sentences, no causal claims.
  • Target 120-180 words - concise but complete.
